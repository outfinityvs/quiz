# Time Horizon Portrait

## Introduction

The Time Horizon Portrait maps how the user relates to past, present, and future, using original items inspired by time perspective concepts.

## Core Content

### Source and Licensing

Inspired by Zimbardo Time Perspective concepts. Items are original (ZTPI is copyrighted and not reproduced).

### Instrument Design

**Question Count:** 15 statements (3 per orientation)
**Response Format:** 5-point Likert scale (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree)
**Duration:** 3-4 minutes
**Scoring:** Sum of agreement scores per orientation

### Dimensions (Time Orientations)

1. **Past-Positive** - Nostalgia, tradition, positive memories, sentimental value
2. **Past-Negative** - Regret, lessons from mistakes, dwelling on past failures
3. **Present-Hedonistic** - Pleasure, spontaneity, living in the moment, sensation-seeking
4. **Present-Fatalistic** - Helplessness, acceptance, belief that future is predetermined
5. **Future-Oriented** - Planning, goals, delayed gratification, future-focused

### Viral Mechanics

- **Time orientation radar:** Visual representation of five orientations
- **Generational comparison:** Compare with parent, child, mentor, mentee
- **Challenge:** "How do you relate to time?" - share quiz URL

### Result Interpretation

Shows positions on five axes describing the user's time orientation. Avoids prescribing "balance" - different orientations serve different life phases.

## Conclusion

Validates Likert scoring and demonstrates generational comparison feature.
