# Energy Economics

## Introduction

The Energy Economics quiz maps what actually drains and recharges the user, using paired-activity choices to measure energy patterns across six dimensions.

## Core Content

### Source and Licensing

Original Outfinity instrument.

### Instrument Design

**Question Count:** 12 paired activities (2 per dimension)
**Response Format:** Forced choice between two activities (which energizes you more?)
**Duration:** 2-3 minutes
**Scoring:** Each choice maps to one energy dimension

### Dimensions (Energy Types)

1. **Social Energy** - People vs. solitude (recharged by social interaction or alone time)
2. **Novelty Energy** - New vs. familiar (recharged by new experiences or routine)
3. **Structure Energy** - Planned vs. spontaneous (recharged by planning or improvisation)
4. **Depth Energy** - Deep vs. broad (recharged by deep focus or variety)
5. **Competition Energy** - Competitive vs. cooperative (recharged by competition or collaboration)
6. **Autonomy Energy** - Independent vs. guided (recharged by independence or mentorship)

### Viral Mechanics

- **Energy ledger:** Visual representation of surplus and deficit across dimensions
- **Team energy audit:** Invite team members to see team energy distribution
- **Comparison:** "Our energy patterns complement each other"

### Result Interpretation

Shows energy surplus/deficit for each dimension as an "energy ledger." Describes what drains and recharges the user.

## Conclusion

Validates paired-choice scoring and demonstrates energy-ledger result format.
