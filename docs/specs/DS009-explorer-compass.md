# Explorer Compass

## Introduction

The Explorer Compass (also known as the Outfinity Circle Quiz) identifies the user's primary and secondary contribution roles, the most compatible venture ecology, complementary collaborators, and a concrete 30-day experiment. It is a reflective exploration published under CC BY 4.0.

## Core Content

The instrument has two sections: 10 forced-choice scenarios (each with first and second preference) that score eight roles, and 8 bipolar ecology trade-offs on five-point Likert scales that score eight venture ecology axes.

The eight roles are: Frontier Scout (FS), Systems Cartographer (SC), Experimental Builder (EB), Mission Operator (MO), Community Weaver (CW), Meaning Translator (MT), Institutional Architect (IA), and Boundary Steward (BS). Each role appears exactly five times across the 10 scenarios. First choice scores 3 points, second choice scores 1 point. Maximum raw score per role is 15.

The eight ecology axes are: Discovery↔Delivery, Autonomy↔Coalition, Speed↔Depth, Rootedness↔Reach, Market↔Commons, External Capital↔Resource Sovereignty, Central Direction↔Distributed Governance, Growth↔Sufficiency. Each is scored on a five-point scale (0, 25, 50, 75, 100).

Eight venture ecology prototypes are defined: Independent Venture Studio, Frontier Research Lab, Mission Startup, Open Commons Network, Community Venture, Cooperative Enterprise, Public-Interest Institution, and Ecosystem Orchestrator. The two closest prototypes (by Euclidean distance) become the primary and adjacent ecologies.

The result includes: role × role title, radar chart, ecology match, contribution, vulnerability, complementarity, experiment, and Explorer Circle invitation. The recommendation appears only after the complete result.

Sharing uses three levels: invite only, minimal result (title + one sentence), and full map (scores + diagram). Comparison ("Compare Our Maps") overlays two results with complementarity analysis.

## Decisions & Questions

### Question #1: Should the ecology axes use bipolar labels or neutral scales?

Response: Bipolar labels (e.g., "Discovery ↔ Delivery") with five positions: Clear A, Rather A, Depends equally, Rather B, Clear B. This forces a directional preference and produces more informative ecology profiles.

### Question #2: Should context questions influence scoring?

Response: No. The optional context questions (current stage, domains of interest) personalize the recommendation text and experiment suggestion but do not affect role or ecology scores.

## Conclusion

The Explorer Compass validates the engine's forced-choice scoring, bipolar Likert scales, prototype distance matching, and complementarity analysis. It is the first quiz and the primary driver for the Explorer Circle community.
